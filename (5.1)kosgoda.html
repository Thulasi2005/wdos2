INTRODUCTION

One of 18 turtle hatcheries along Sri Lanka's southern coast is the Turtle Care Centre at Kosgoda Beach. The institution was founded in 1981 and is situated in the seaside community of Kosgoda in South Western Australia. Additionally, Kosgoda serves as the nesting ground for all five species of turtles that come to Sri Lanka. This is not the case with many of the other island-area hatcheries, where just some of the species are present on the beaches.


The facility primarily serves as a hatchery and makes great efforts to boost the rate of hatching and survival of young turtles. However, the Care Centre also treats and releases ill or wounded individuals back into the water after treating them. They also organise awareness campaigns for both domestic and foreign tourists and volunteer programmes.


HISTORY

The project started by Dudley Perera in 1988. The project's major goals are to keep an eye on local sea turtle activities and protect nearby nesting areas. We want people to understand how vulnerable these lovely animals are and how crucial it is to contribute to their protection before it's too late.


The project's hatchery is one of its most vital components. Before being reintroduced into the water, saved and gathered eggs can safely develop within the project's sanctuary away from scavengers. Additionally, a specific number of each hatching is withheld for a brief time for "headstarting" prior to release.


The goal of the hatchery programme is to increase the number of hatchlings that make it to the ocean and endure the perilous early stages of life.


PROJECT WORKS
  • BEACH CONSERVATION:
    --The preservation of turtle nesting areas is essential for the successful reproduction of the species. This entails maintaining a clean beach and adding shelter plants.

  • PROJECT OPERATIONS:
    --To continue running, the initiative needs support from both the local community and volunteers. The project must be kept neat and orderly, visitors must be taken on a tour of the grounds, the turtle tanks must be well-maintained, and the turtles must be fed and cared for.

  • EDICATION:
    --Volunteers will have the opportunity to participate in giving tours of the project and imparting some of their newly acquired information to residents and visitors. The conservation of these intriguing animals depends on public understanding of them, their threats, and how to protect them.
